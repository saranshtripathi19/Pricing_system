# Noon Strategic Pricing Optimizer (SPO) 🚀

### *From Reactive Repricing to Strategic Yield Management*

The **Strategic Pricing Optimizer (SPO)** is an internal-only decision-making engine designed for Noon. Unlike standard "match-the-lowest" repricers, the SPO utilizes a **Multi-Objective Optimization Engine** to align SKU-level pricing with high-level business intent.

## 🎯 The Problem Statement

Managing millions of SKUs across the UAE, KSA, and Egypt in real-time is humanly impossible. The SPO addresses:

  * **Margin Leakage:** Recovers lost profit by identifying when customers are willing to pay more.
  * **Buy Box Attrition:** Eliminates the latency between a competitor's price drop and our response.
  * **Inventory Deadstock:** Prevents capital from being locked in slow-moving items through automated price decay.

-----

## 🏛️ The Five Strategic Pillars

Every price generated by the engine is synthesized from five foundational themes:

1.  **Market Synchronicity:** Real-time ingestion of competitor signals (Amazon, local giants).
2.  **Adaptive Elasticity:** Predicting volume shifts using historical behavior models.
3.  **Yield Management:** Linking price directly to "Days of Cover" (DoC).
4.  **Financial Governance:** Strict safety floors to ensure unit economics remain healthy.
5.  **Strategy Simulation:** A sandbox environment for "What-If" impact analysis.

## 🧪 Technical Architecture (The Math)

The SPO doesn't guess; it calculates. Below are the primary algorithms driving the engine:

### **A. Competitor Signal (k-NN)**

We use **Weighted k-Nearest Neighbors** to filter the market. The system ignores competitors with delivery times \>3 days or low seller ratings, focusing only on "True Competitors."

### **B. Demand Elasticity (Log-Log Regression)**

The system calculates the **Price Elasticity of Demand ($E_d$)** to find the revenue-maximizing price:
$$E_d = \frac{\% \Delta \text{ Quantity Demanded}}{\% \Delta \text{ Price}}$$
If $E_d$ is low, the model pushes for **Margin Harvest**.

### **C. Yield Management (PID Controller)**

To clear stock, we use a **Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID)** controller. It measures the "error" between current stock levels and target "Days of Cover," forcing price pressure to hit velocity targets.

### **D. Final Optimization (Thompson Sampling)**

A **Reinforcement Learning** approach that "experiments" with price points on a small traffic segment to discover the highest reward based on the selected Goal (Orders vs. Margin).

## 🛡️ Governance & Circuit Breakers

To protect the Noon brand and P\&L, the SPO includes mandatory logic gates:

| Guardrail | Logic | Trigger |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Minimum Margin Floor** | $Price \ge Cost + 2\%$ | Always active unless manual override. |
| **Race-to-Bottom Break** | Freeze Pricing | Triggered if $>4$ price changes occur in $<60$ mins. |
| **Fair Price Check** | Hide from Search | Triggered if price is significantly higher than market mean. |
